# 🎬 Netflix Business Case Study: Content Strategy & Data Analysis

## 📌 Overview
This project analyzes Netflix’s content dataset to uncover insights about content distribution, trends, and strategic opportunities. The goal is to understand how Netflix’s content strategy varies across genres, regions, and time.

---

## 🎯 Problem Statement
Netflix aims to optimize its content strategy to maximize user engagement and retention. This analysis focuses on:

- What type of content dominates the platform?
- How has content production evolved over time?
- Which genres and countries contribute the most content?
- What insights can guide future content investment decisions?

---

## 📊 Dataset Description
The dataset contains information about Netflix titles, including:

- Title
- Type (Movie / TV Show)
- Genre
- Director & Cast
- Country
- Release year
- Date added to platform
- Duration

---

## 🧪 Methodology

### 1. Data Cleaning & Preprocessing
- Handled missing values
- Standardized categorical variables
- Processed date and time-related features

### 2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Analyzed distribution of movies vs TV shows
- Studied genre and country-wise content trends
- Examined yearly content growth patterns
- Visualized insights using charts and graphs

### 3. Trend Analysis
- Evaluated content addition trends over time
- Identified dominant genres and regions
- Compared content strategies across categories

---

## 📈 Key Insights

- 🎥 Movies dominate Netflix’s content library compared to TV shows
- 🌍 Certain countries contribute significantly more content than others
- 📈 Content production has increased rapidly in recent years
- 🎭 Specific genres consistently outperform others in volume

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

- Invest more in high-performing genres and regions
- Increase focus on original content in growing markets
- Balance movie and TV show production for engagement
- Use data-driven insights to guide future content acquisition
